About

➞ What is CatForge?

CatForge is Texas State University’s premier software development lab. Every semester, we place Computer Science, Communication Design, and Business Administration students onto development teams to create software projects from the ground up.

➞ Team Structure

Teams consist of:

  • 6 Student Developers
  • 1 Student Designers
  • 1 Student Project Manager
  • 1 Industry Mentor

➞ Symposium Night

At the end of the semester, each team presents their project to other CatForge projects teams, Texas State students and staff, and industry sponsors. These collectives vote on the winning project based on scope, execution, technical difficulty, and presentation, and 1st and 2nd place winning teams receive an award for their work.

FAQ

Is this program open to non-CS majors?

Yes! CatForge mirrors the way real software startups push projects into production, which means we need students from all fields to serve not only as developers, but project managers and designers. As long as you are prepared to learn about basic technical concepts, you are welcome to apply!

Who are the industry sponsors?

Industry sponsors are Texas State graduates further ahead in their career who will provide mentorship and project guidance. They meet with teams once every 2 weeks. Full list of industry sponsors can be found here.

What is the time commitment like?

CatForge projects can take up a significant amount of time depending on what your role in the project is and what you put into it. You can expect to spend at minimum 10 hrs/week doing project-related tasks.

Do I need prior development experience?

No prior development experience is necessary! We are aiming this program towards students who have little to no experience :)

I only have online classes / I have a long commute — can I participate virtually?

Yes! We have hybrid development teams and virtual-only teams. As long as you can participate in symposium night and our two socials per semester in-person, virtual attendees are welcome.

Why should I join?

CatForge projects mirror the life cycle that occurs in real-world software development. By joining a team of developers, not only are you creating a project for your resume with a group of new friends, but you’re also preparing yourself for handling projects during your career.
